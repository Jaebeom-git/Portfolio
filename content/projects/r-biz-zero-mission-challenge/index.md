R-BIZ ZERO Mission Challenge에서 ZEUS 6축 산업용 로봇과 비전 카메라를 이용해 기계식 키보드 키캡 조립 미션을 수행한 경진대회 프로젝트입니다.

## Overview

This competition project developed a vision-guided robotic keycap assembly system for the R-BIZ ZERO Mission Challenge. The system used a ZEUS industrial robot, an overhead vision camera, and a suction-based end-effector to recognize keycap positions and assemble keyboard keycaps under the mission constraints.

The project connected camera-based object localization, robot motion planning, end-effector operation, and task-sequence control. The final system reached the national final round and was later organized as a domestic conference poster on image-based keyboard keycap assembly.

## Contributions

- Built the vision-guided workflow for detecting keyboard keycap positions from camera images.
- Implemented robot motion sequences for pick-and-place keycap assembly using a ZEUS industrial manipulator.
- Integrated a low-cost suction-based end-effector with the assembly routine and tested task repeatability.
- Analyzed practical limitations caused by illumination changes, vision robustness, suction reliability, and assembly speed.
- Participated in the R-BIZ ZERO Mission Challenge final round and received First Prize at the 2022 Mechatronics Engineering Capstone Design Fair & Exhibition.

#### Abstract

Vision-based robot assembly can reduce the need for fixed mechanical fixtures by allowing the robot to identify object positions from camera images. This presentation developed a keyboard keycap assembly robot for the R-BIZ ZERO Mission Challenge. The system recognized keycap locations using image processing, generated robot motion commands for pick-and-place assembly, and used a suction-based end-effector to place keycaps on the keyboard. The project also identified practical constraints in lighting robustness, suction stability, and task-cycle time during competition operation.
